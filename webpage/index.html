As climate change increases the frequency, intensity, and duration of heatwaves, understanding their impact on electricity grids is crucial for enhancing societal security and resilience.

Heatwave Crisis in Europe

Heatwaves are defined as periods when the maximum temperature exceeded the 98th percentile of the 1961-1990 reference period, and exceeded 28°C, for a period of three or more days.

Recent Significant Events

  • 2019: The Western European Heatwaves ECMWF Analysis →
  • 2022: Extreme Heat Crisis Copernicus Climate Report →
  • 2023: July Heatwave Surge ECMWF Science Blog →

These events demonstrate increasing frequency and intensity of extreme heat across Europe, with significant impacts on health, agriculture, and infrastructure.

Impacts on Electricity Grid

Risk Factors

  • ⬆ Cooling Demand: Peak consumption during extreme heat
  • ⬇ Generation Capacity: Efficiency drops as temperatures rise
  • ⬇ Transmission Capacity: Overloaded lines and equipment

Heatwaves-induced imbalanced demand and supply significantly increase the risk of power grid load shedding and blackouts

Methodology

To evaluate the impacts of heatwaves on electricity grids, we propose a comprehensive methodology that includes:

  • Future Heatwave Projection (2025-2030)
  • Future Demand Modeling by Demand.ninja
  • Renewable Generation Analysis by Atlite
  • Generation Derating Analysis
  • Multi-Segment Conductor Thermal Modeling
  • AC Optimal Power Flow Analysis
