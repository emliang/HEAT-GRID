HEAT-GRID

A comprehensive study of heatwave effects on European electricity grids. As climate change increases the frequency, intensity, and duration of heatwaves, understanding their impact on electricity grids is crucial for enhancing societal security and resilience.

Heatwave Crisis in Europe

Heatwaves are defined as periods when the maximum temperature exceeded the 98th percentile of the 1961-1990 reference period, and exceeded 28°C, for a period of three or more days.

Recent Significant Events

Key Datasets

Our research leverages multiple authoritative data sources:

  • PyPSA-Eur: European transmission network data
  • ERA5: Historical climate data
  • C3S: Future climate projections
  • ENTSO-E: Power demand statistics
  • Demand.ninja: Weather-dependent demand models
  • Atlite: Renewable generation calculations
